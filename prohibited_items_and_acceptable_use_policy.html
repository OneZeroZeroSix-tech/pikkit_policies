Effective Date: January 10, 2026

Last Updated: January 10, 2026

1. PURPOSE AND LEGAL FUNCTION OF THIS POLICY

This Prohibited Items & Acceptable Use Policy (“Policy” or “AUP”) is a standalone, binding policy governing all use of the Pykkit platform, including but not limited to the website, mobile applications, APIs, communication tools, and any related services (collectively, the “Platform”).

The purpose of this Policy is to:

  • Protect the safety of Couriers, Senders, Receivers, and the public

  • Prevent criminal, illegal, or harmful use of the Platform

  • Establish clear, enforceable rules for acceptable conduct

  • Enable swift suspension or termination of accounts

  • Reduce legal, regulatory, and insurance exposure

  • Demonstrate good-faith compliance with U.S. federal, state, and local laws

This Policy is intentionally separate from the Terms of Service to ensure clarity, enforceability, and ease of reference. Courts, regulators, and insurers routinely expect a clear, standalone Acceptable Use Policy for platforms that facilitate physical goods transportation.

The Platform operates under a zero-tolerance policy for prohibited items and prohibited uses.

5. GENERAL PROHIBITED USES OF THE PLATFORM

Users may not use the Platform to:

  • Commit, facilitate, or conceal illegal activity

  • Transport items prohibited by law or this Policy

  • Evade law enforcement, taxes, or regulatory oversight

  • Harass, threaten, or endanger others

  • Misrepresent the nature of items or deliveries

  • Circumvent Platform safeguards, monitoring, or fees

Any attempt to exploit the Platform for unlawful or harmful purposes constitutes a material breach.

6. ABSOLUTELY PROHIBITED ITEMS (NON-EXHAUSTIVE)

The following categories of items are strictly prohibited under all circumstances, regardless of jurisdiction, intent, or quantity.

6.1 Controlled Substances and Drugs

  • Illegal drugs, narcotics, or controlled substances

  • Marijuana where prohibited or improperly authorized

  • Prescription drugs without valid prescriptions

  • Drug paraphernalia intended for illegal use

Explanation: Transporting controlled substances exposes the Platform and Couriers to severe criminal liability, including felony charges, asset forfeiture, and incarceration.

6.2 Weapons and Explosives

  • Firearms of any kind

  • Ammunition

  • Explosives, fireworks, or detonators

  • Firearm parts or accessories

  • Knives or weapons restricted by law

Explanation: Weapons transport is heavily regulated and presents extreme safety risks. Unauthorized transport can result in felony charges and civil liability.

6.3 Hazardous and Dangerous Materials

  • Flammable liquids or gases

  • Toxic chemicals

  • Radioactive materials

  • Corrosive substances

  • Biohazardous waste

Explanation: These items require specialized handling, licensing, and insurance not provided by the Platform.

6.4 Stolen, Counterfeit, or Illicit Goods

  • Stolen property

  • Counterfeit products

  • Pirated media

  • Goods obtained through fraud

Explanation: Transporting illicit goods may implicate Users and the Platform in criminal enterprises.

6.5 Human Trafficking and Exploitation Materials

  • Any form of human trafficking facilitation

  • Sexual exploitation materials

  • Child sexual abuse materials (CSAM)

Explanation: Zero tolerance. Immediate termination and mandatory law enforcement reporting.

6.6 Living Beings

  • Humans

  • Animals (alive or deceased), except where expressly authorized in writing

  • Biological specimens

Explanation: Transport of living beings presents ethical, safety, and legal risks.

7. CONDITIONALLY PROHIBITED ITEMS

Certain items may be prohibited depending on jurisdiction, quantity, packaging, or authorization.

Examples include:

  • Alcohol

  • Tobacco products

  • Perishable food

  • High-value electronics

  • Medical supplies

Senders must verify legality and compliance before submitting delivery requests.

8. MISREPRESENTATION AND FRAUD

Users may not:

  • Mislabel items

  • Understate weight or size

  • Conceal prohibited contents

  • Provide false documentation

Explanation: Misrepresentation undermines safety, voids insurance coverage, and constitutes grounds for immediate termination.

9. COURIER OBLIGATIONS REGARDING PROHIBITED ITEMS

Couriers must:

  • Decline deliveries suspected of violating this Policy

  • Refuse items that appear unsafe or illegal

  • Report suspected violations immediately

  • Not proceed under pressure or coercion

Couriers are never required to complete a delivery that appears unsafe or unlawful.
