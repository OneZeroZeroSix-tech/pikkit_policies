Pikkit – Law Enforcement Cooperation & Regulatory Compliance Policy

Effective Date: January 10, 2026

Governing Law: State of California, United States

Last Updated: January 10, 2026

1. PURPOSE AND LEGAL INTENT

This Law Enforcement Cooperation & Regulatory Compliance Policy (“Policy”) establishes the Platform’s authority, obligations, and procedures regarding cooperation with federal, state, and local law enforcement agencies and regulatory bodies.

The objectives of this Policy are to:

  • Prevent criminal misuse of the Platform

  • Comply with applicable U.S. federal and state laws

  • Protect public safety

  • Limit Platform liability and regulatory exposure

  • Provide clear notice to Users regarding data disclosure and cooperation

This Policy is incorporated by reference into the Terms of Service, Prohibited Items & Acceptable Use Policy, Courier Independent Contractor Agreement, and all related agreements.

2. NO EXPECTATION OF PRIVACY FOR ILLEGAL ACTIVITY

Users acknowledge and agree that:

  • The Platform does not guarantee confidentiality for activity involving illegal conduct

  • Communications, delivery data, account information, and transactional records may be reviewed, preserved, or disclosed where legally required or reasonably necessary to prevent harm

  • The Platform expressly disclaims any obligation to shield Users from law enforcement inquiries arising from their own actions

This Policy does not waive constitutional or statutory rights but provides advance notice of lawful cooperation.

3. GENERAL AUTHORITY TO COOPERATE WITH LAW ENFORCEMENT

The Platform may, without notice to the User where legally permitted:

  • Respond to subpoenas, warrants, court orders, and lawful requests

  • Voluntarily disclose information when the Platform reasonably believes such disclosure is necessary to prevent death, serious bodily harm, or significant property damage

  • Preserve records pending investigation

  • Suspend or terminate accounts implicated in unlawful activity

4. CONTROLLED SUBSTANCES ACT (CSA) COMPLIANCE

(21 U.S.C. §§ 801–971)

The Platform strictly prohibits the use of its services to manufacture, distribute, dispense, transport, or possess controlled substances in violation of the Controlled Substances Act (“CSA”).

4.1 Reporting and Disclosure

Where the Platform becomes aware of activity reasonably suspected to violate the CSA, it may:

  • Report such activity to the Drug Enforcement Administration (DEA) or other appropriate authorities

  • Preserve delivery records, communications, IP addresses, and account identifiers

  • Disclose User information as required by subpoena or exigent circumstances

4.2 No Safe Harbor for Misrepresentation

Mislabeling, concealment, or misrepresentation of controlled substances constitutes a material violation of Platform policies and may result in immediate termination and referral to law enforcement.

5. BUREAU OF ALCOHOL, TOBACCO, FIREARMS AND EXPLOSIVES (ATF) COMPLIANCE

(18 U.S.C. Chapter 44; 27 C.F.R. Parts 447, 555)

The Platform prohibits any delivery or facilitation involving firearms, ammunition, explosives, or regulated weapons except where explicitly permitted by law and expressly authorized in writing by the Platform.

5.1 ATF Cooperation

The Platform may cooperate with the ATF by:

  • Providing transaction data related to suspected unlawful weapons transport

  • Identifying accounts involved in prohibited firearms activity

  • Preserving evidence for criminal investigation

5.2 Zero Tolerance Enforcement

Any suspected ATF-regulated violation may result in:

  • Immediate account suspension

  • Permanent termination

  • Law enforcement referral without prior notice

6. DEPARTMENT OF TRANSPORTATION (DOT) COMPLIANCE

(49 U.S.C. Subtitle VI; 49 C.F.R.)

The Platform enforces compliance with DOT regulations governing transportation safety, hazardous materials, and vehicle operation.

6.1 Hazardous Materials (HAZMAT)

The Platform prohibits the transportation of hazardous materials requiring DOT placarding, registration, or special handling unless expressly authorized.

Where suspected HAZMAT violations occur, the Platform may:

  • Notify DOT or Federal Motor Carrier Safety Administration (FMCSA)

  • Preserve vehicle, route, and shipment data

  • Suspend Courier access pending investigation

6.2 Vehicle Safety and Licensing

Couriers remain solely responsible for compliance with DOT licensing, registration, and safety requirements. The Platform may cooperate with DOT authorities regarding unsafe operations.

7. TRANSPORTATION SECURITY ADMINISTRATION (TSA) CONSIDERATIONS

(49 U.S.C. Chapter 114)

Although the Platform does not operate as an air carrier, it prohibits any attempt to:

  • Circumvent TSA security requirements

  • Transport items prohibited from air or secure transportation channels

  • Use the Platform to evade aviation or transportation security laws

Where relevant, the Platform may coordinate with TSA or related agencies regarding security threats or prohibited items.

8. EMERGENCY DISCLOSURES AND EXIGENT CIRCUMSTANCES

The Platform may disclose User information without prior legal process where it reasonably believes disclosure is necessary to:

  • Prevent imminent harm

  • Respond to credible threats

  • Assist emergency responders

  • Protect national or public security

Such disclosures are made in good faith and consistent with applicable law.

9. DATA PRESERVATION AND RETENTION

Upon receipt of a lawful request or upon reasonable belief of criminal activity, the Platform may:

  • Preserve User data beyond standard retention periods

  • Restrict account access

  • Maintain audit logs for investigatory purposes

Preservation does not imply wrongdoing but ensures legal compliance.

10. USER NOTIFICATION

Where permitted by law, the Platform may notify Users of law enforcement requests. Notification may be delayed or withheld where:

  • Prohibited by law

  • Necessary to prevent harm

  • Likely to compromise an investigation

11. NO LIABILITY FOR GOOD-FAITH COOPERATION

Users agree that the Platform shall not be liable for:

  • Disclosure of information made in good faith

  • Account actions taken to comply with legal obligations

  • Cooperation with law enforcement or regulatory agencies

This protection applies to the fullest extent permitted by law.

12. INDEMNIFICATION

Users agree to indemnify and hold harmless the Platform from claims arising out of:

  • Law enforcement investigations caused by User conduct

  • Regulatory penalties attributable to User violations

  • Third-party claims related to illegal activity

13. POLICY UPDATES

This Policy may be updated to reflect changes in law, enforcement priorities, or regulatory guidance. Continued use of the Platform constitutes acceptance.

14. ACKNOWLEDGMENT

By accessing or using the Platform, Users acknowledge that:

  • They understand this Policy

  • They consent to lawful cooperation with authorities

  • They assume all risk associated with illegal or prohibited conduct
